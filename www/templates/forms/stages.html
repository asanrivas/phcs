Pressure Ulcer Staging
Staging of Pressure Ulcers is done
ONCE only on admission or
when new ulcers occur
Stage I:
Superficial layers of the epidermis are
intact but there is erythema
or discolouration

Stage II:
Partial thickness loss involving epidermis and/or dermis.
The ulcer is superficial and presents Clinically
as an abrasion, blister, or shallow

Stage III:
Full thickness skin loss involving
subcutaneous tissue that may estend
down to underlying tascia. The ulcer
may present as a deep crater
with or without undermining of
adjacent tissues.
